Research

Understanding the neural syntax that lets the brain compute information.

I use bottom up engineering and top down neuroscience together to build a more holistic map of how neural circuits organize, represent, and transform information.

Framework

Bottom up engineering

Build controlled neural systems in vitro to understand how circuits form, adapt, and reorganize under defined perturbations and interfaces.

Top down neuroscience

Identify how circuit-level activity patterns drive behavior, then use those insights to target mechanisms and measurements in engineered preparations.

These approaches constrain each other, linking controlled circuit construction to real neural computations.

Engineer in vitro neural networks in constrained geometries to test how connectivity and plasticity emerge under tightly controlled physical and biochemical conditions.

Develop nanoscale neural interfaces to probe and modulate local circuit signaling, with emphasis on measurable mechanisms that can transfer to larger-scale neuroscience questions.

Top down neuroscience

Extract interpretable population-level features from neural recordings to quantify state transitions, ensemble structure, and behavior-linked activity motifs.

Determine how circuit motifs support action selection and adaptive performance in freely moving paradigms, and use those insights to prioritize mechanistic tests.

Integration Goal

Closing the loop across scales

These tracks are intentionally interdependent: engineered neural systems provide mechanistic leverage, and behavioral circuit analyses provide ecological constraints. The long-term goal is a bidirectional framework that improves both explanatory depth and practical translational utility.
