About

Building practical neuroscience that connects neural dynamics to behavior.

I am a neuroscientist and engineer focused on how neural population activity and internal state shape behavior. My work combines quantitative analysis, experimental neuroscience, and instrument development for freely moving systems.

Brief Statement

Research perspective

I am interested in the neural basis of adaptive behavior, with emphasis on population-level signaling, sleep and state-dependent processing, and mechanisms of circuit dysfunction. I approach these questions by blending calcium imaging, electrophysiology-aligned analysis, and computational modeling.

A core part of my work is building tools that make previously hard-to-measure dynamics accessible, reproducible, and shareable across labs.

Background

How I work

  • Design experiments around ethologically relevant behavior and internal-state transitions.
  • Use computational pipelines to convert large neural recordings into interpretable circuit-level features.
  • Prototype hardware and software tools that improve throughput, repeatability, and data quality.
  • Prioritize open, reusable resources for the broader neuroscience community.
