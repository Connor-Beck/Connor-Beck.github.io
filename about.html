About

Engineering neuroscience to decode neural dynamics and build practical tools for behavior research.

I work at the intersection of systems neuroscience and neuroengineering, with a focus on how population-level signaling and internal state shape adaptive behavior.

My approach combines quantitative neural analysis, experiment design in freely moving systems, and hands-on development of open software and laboratory platforms. I aim to make advanced methods more accessible, reproducible, and usable across labs.

Research Perspective

How I frame problems

I am interested in the mechanisms that link neural activity to action selection, internal-state transitions, and long-timescale adaptation. I focus on analyses that move from single-neuron descriptions toward interpretable circuit-level features.

  • Track neural population motifs across behavior, arousal, and sleep-related dynamics.
  • Build analysis pipelines that remain transparent and testable across datasets.
  • Integrate engineering constraints early so methods stay practical for real experiments.

Working Style

What I prioritize

  • Quantitative rigor with reproducible code and clear validation checkpoints.
  • Experimental systems that scale from pilot studies to robust behavioral datasets.
  • Open resources that help trainees and collaborators build faster.
  • Tool choices that balance scientific value with practical lab constraints.

Current Focus Areas

Population activity and state

Characterizing how network-level activity motifs shift across behavior, arousal, and sleep-like transitions.

Circuit dysfunction and resilience

Investigating signatures of altered signaling in conditions linked to neurodegeneration and cognitive decline.

Neuroscience tool development

Building and sharing practical software and laboratory platforms that accelerate behavioral neuroscience experiments.
